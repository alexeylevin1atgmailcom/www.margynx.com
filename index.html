What makes repricing different

Not a blind repricer

Agentix does not chase the cheapest possible price. It balances Buy Box probability, margin protection, traffic and conversion signals, inventory exposure, and the business context around each cohort.

  • Floor and guardrail logic for controlled moves
  • Automation only where the conditions justify it
  • Ability to push price upward again once share stabilizes
Explainable operations

Executive summaries, not analyst homework

The cockpit speaks in natural business language. A seller can understand the story quickly without needing deep BI, spreadsheet modeling, or marketplace forensics.

  • Why it likely changed

AI command center

Ask the cockpit the way you would ask a strong analyst or seller operator.

The interface is meant to feel human and direct. The seller does not need to translate business intent into a complex workflow.

Business analyst mode

“Why did margin drop by 5% last week for this product group, and what should I do to recover it?”

Seller operations mode

“Reduce the floor price by 1.2% for this product list and watch whether Buy Box share improves without hurting margin too much.”

Automation mode

“Enable repricing automation for this list to win more Buy Box, then work prices back up toward 10% where orders stay healthy.”

Guardrail mode

“If higher prices start reducing units, stop the increase and keep the previous safe price level.”
